The Tuekta site was selected as a test area. The site, situated along the River Ursul, has been famous since the 1950s. It has 197 burial mounds and surrounding side structures  and the main complex appears to be a row of five monumental Scythian burial mounds with diameters between 42 and 76 m. Two of these were excavated by S.I. Rudenko and his team in  1954 (Rudenko, 1960, 1970). Since then, numerous excavations have taken place on the site, although most have remained unpublished. The burial mounds are related to the well-known  Pazyryk culture, dating from 5the3rd century BCE (Parzinger, 2006: 606). The site is located in an agricultural zone and many of the mounds have been plundered or excavated. Grasses, shrubs  and trees are growing on top of the monuments
